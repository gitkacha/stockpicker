Strict laws to regulate LLMs would do more harm than good. First, the pace of AI innovation far outstrips legislative processes, meaning any strict regulations would be obsolete by implementation. Second, such regulations would entrench power in big tech companies that can afford compliance teams, stifling competition and innovation from smaller players. Third, the problems attributed to LLMs—harmful content, bias, privacy concerns—are already addressed by existing legal frameworks. Rather than creating new, complex regulations that might have unintended consequences, we should focus on adapting current laws and allowing market pressures to drive responsible development. The solution to potential misuse lies in technical innovation and industry standards, not in legal mandates that would create regulatory fragmentation across jurisdictions and potentially restrict beneficial applications like scientific research and creative expression. Freedom to innovate must be balanced with responsibility, but heavy-handed regulation would tip that scale too far toward restriction.
